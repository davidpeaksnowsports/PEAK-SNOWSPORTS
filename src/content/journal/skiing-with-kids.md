The Portes du Soleil is one of the best ski areas in the world for families, and Morzine and Avoriaz are at the heart of it. Gentle beginner slopes, exciting kids' zones, and plenty of off-snow fun.

Here's how to make skiing with kids in these resorts fun, safe, and stress-free.

## Choose the right resort for your family

**Morzine**, a charming Alpine village with a warm, family-friendly atmosphere. The beginner slopes at the Pleney are perfect for young skiers, and the village has everything from sledging areas to child-friendly restaurants.

**Avoriaz**, a car-free, ski-in/ski-out resort where you can step out of your accommodation straight onto the snow. It's home to **The Lil' Stash**, a magical tree-run park full of fun features and huts, and wide, gentle beginner zones perfect for children building confidence.

## Book lessons with the right instructors

Kids learn best when they're having fun. We use games, gentle challenges, and lots of encouragement to keep young skiers engaged.

Benefits of booking kids' lessons in Morzine and Avoriaz:

- Small groups, more personal attention
- English-speaking instructors
- Safety-first approach with expert supervision
- Lessons that make learning feel like play
- Free time to hit some blacks and bumps, while leaving your kids in competent hands, or book a lesson for yourself to refine that technique

## Dress for success (and comfort)

Alpine weather can change quickly, so layering is key. Make sure your child has:

- Waterproof jacket and salopettes
- Thermal base layers
- Warm socks
- Neck warmer
- Goggles and sunglasses
- Well-fitting helmet

Hot chocolate breaks are just as important as ski runs, warm kids are happy kids.

## Keep it manageable

A full day on the slopes can be too much for little legs. Consider:

- Starting with half-day lessons, [check out our kids' groups](/lessons/family)
- Building in plenty of breaks
- Mixing ski time with other activities: sledging in Morzine, or the Aquariaz water park in Avoriaz

## Après-ski, kid style

Après-ski isn't just for adults. In Morzine, head to the ice rink in the village square or grab a crêpe at one of the many cafés. In Avoriaz, kids love watching the horse-drawn sleighs and exploring the snowy pedestrian streets.

## Final tip

Skiing with kids in Morzine and Avoriaz is all about balance, between learning and playing, between challenge and comfort. With the right preparation and the right instructors, it's the perfect place for your children to fall in love with skiing, and for you to make unforgettable family memories in the heart of the Portes du Soleil.
