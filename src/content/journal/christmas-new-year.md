There's something truly special about spending the festive season in the Alps. Snow-dusted chalets, twinkling Christmas decorations, and that fresh snow underfoot. If you're dreaming of a white Christmas or celebrating the New Year on skis, Morzine and Avoriaz are the perfect places to celebrate, and the Peak team are here to make it an experience to remember.

## Christmas in the Alps, pure magic

Imagine waking up on Christmas morning to fresh snowflakes falling outside your chalet, the smell of croissants and coffee drifting through the air, and the mountains glowing under the winter sun.

In **Morzine**, the village transforms into a winter wonderland. Fairy lights along the streets, ice-skating in the square, and Santa making appearances on the slopes. The festive markets are full of local treats and gifts, perfect for a cosy Christmas Eve stroll after skiing.

Up in **Avoriaz**, the car-free streets are covered in snow. Families love the horse-drawn sleigh rides through the village, and the magical evening torchlight descents over on Pleney.

## Christmas lessons with Peak

Whether it's your child's first time on skis or you're fine-tuning your carving before the Christmas Day feast, our instructors are here to help you make the most of your festive ski time.

Private and group lessons run throughout the holidays, so you can balance time on the mountain with festive fun in the village. Expect laughter, confidence-building, and proper ski technique.

## New Year on the slopes

If you've never celebrated New Year's Eve in the Alps, you're in for a treat.

In **Morzine**, the main square comes alive with music, fireworks, and dancing under the stars. Families gather for hot chocolates and mulled wine, while the countdown plays out against the backdrop of the Pleney slopes.

Meanwhile, **Avoriaz** hosts a legendary New Year celebration high above the valley, live DJs, dancing, and fireworks lighting up the snow-covered peaks.

And on New Year's Day? There's no better way to start the year and ski off the morning grogginess than with a few crisp morning turns.
